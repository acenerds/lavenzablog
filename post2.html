On... being an orphan

4/23/26

Am I qualified to write about this? I like to think I am! I mean, who else is an orphan, huh? Batman, Spider-Man, Nelson Mandela, Alexander Hamilton, and Lavenza (me). Well Leonardo and Da Vinci aren't here to tell you about it, so I am!

The harshest truth that we all kind of have to accept? The goal of life IS to be an orphan. Every parent would rather their child become an orphan than they become whatever the name is of a parent who lost a child. The difference is age. When you parents die while you're 40 or 50, that's sad but like, expected. When you're parents die when you're 14, before you've had your first kiss, when you still don't know how to cook properly or shave without cutting your legs or drive or anything, that's when it's a bit more unfair. I don't mean any offense to any adults who have lost their parents, but I feel like you're more prepared for it. In Naruto, Sasuke has this idea that Naruto can't possibly understand his pain of being an orphan because he (Sasuke) at least knew and loved his family while Naruto has never known any parents or family (unless you count Iruka-sensei). I think that's unfair, but I can also track the logic. I think if you were born and your parents were dead, or they died before you could really form memories, that is tragic. If your parents died while you're already an adult and maybe even a parent yourself, that is tragic. If your parents died when you're just starting your first year of high school, that's like, super duper tragic and unfair.

As much as I like to complain, oh woe is me, poor Lavenza with poor dead parents, I also kind of feel like this is always been my life. It's a bit odd to think, when I turn 29 I will have lived longer without my parents than I had with them, which, if we're not counting before I was like sentient and aware, the gap is even wider.
