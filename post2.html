On... being an orphan

4/23/26

Am I qualified to write about this? I like to think I am! I mean, who else is an orphan, huh? Batman, Spider-Man, Nelson Mandela, Alexander Hamilton, and Lavenza (me). Well Leonardo and Da Vinci aren't here to tell you about it, so I am!

The harshest truth that we all kind of have to accept? The goal of life IS to be an orphan. Every parent would rather their child become an orphan than they become whatever the name is of a parent who lost a child. The difference is age. When you parents die while you're 40 or 50, that's sad but like, expected. When you're parents die when you're 14, before you've had your first kiss, when you still don't know how to cook properly or shave without cutting your legs or drive or anything, that's when it's a bit more unfair. I don't mean any offense to any adults who have lost their parents, but I feel like you're more prepared for it.

As much as I like to complain, oh woe is me, poor Lavenza with poor dead parents, I also kind of feel like this is always been my life. It's a bit odd to think, when I turn 29 I will have lived longer without my parents than I had with them, which, if we're not counting before I was like sentient and aware, the gap is even wider. I honestly don't entirely know what it is in particular that I am "lacking" or "missing". On the surface, it's obvious, I'm lacking parents. But it's more like, I can't name or place my finger on exactly what I am missing or have lost. That absence that' there, it's not something that can really be named in my opinion. It's like, the shape of my life is just fundamentally different and there isn't any way to really pin down why or what's going on.

In Naruto, Sasuke has this idea that Naruto can't possibly understand his pain of being an orphan because he (Sasuke) at least knew and loved his family while Naruto has never known any parents or family (unless you count Iruka-sensei). I think that's unfair, but I can also track the logic. There’s a difference between losing something and never having had it, but I don’t think one cancels out the other. They’re just… different shapes of the same problem.

There's layers to this, at least I feel like there are. I think if your parents died before you could form memories, that is tragic. If your parents died while you're already an adult and maybe even a parent yourself, that is tragic. If your parents died when you're just starting your first year of high school, it's just so insanely unfair. I think I kind of agree, I would rather be Naruto who never had parents than Sasuke, who loved and lost parents. Of course that's not to say I'm not grateful for having parents at all, I obviously did like knowing them.

Unfortunately, as you'll come to notice about these cute little "On..." segments, I don't really have any particularly neat conclusion to wrap everything up in. Sorry if that's what you were looking for. But that's mainly because, real life isn't a story. Things just sort of happen and sure you CAN search for meaning in them, but I've alays been of the opinion that my parents died because people die, not to implant within me some beautiful and deep life lesson about love and loss. I don't know which would be worse though, a meaningless death, or a death that only happened for another person to grow. But yeah, being an orphan isn't really a singular experience so I am kind of just talking for myself here.
