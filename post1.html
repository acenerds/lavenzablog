The First of Many (hopefully)

4/23/26

I am writing this instead of doing my homework today. I have an anthropology exam tommorow and I have classes from 3:50 all the way until 8:40. Isnt't that so evil? There's two seriously cute girls in my English class though and it's a discussion based class so I always try to talk as much as I can and brag about how intellectual I am. One of them thinks I'm very funny so I'll take a win when I can.

Today I volunteered at a middle school, it's part of a community service thing for my english class. The class is all about teaching English and there's a bit of a problem with that. In class we discuss things like helping people write and revise their essays or teaching them to read more analytically but at the middle school all I'm really doing is telling kids to stop the side chatter or helping them with math homework! When my professor asks about how we're implementing the strategies we learned from our readings, I don't have an answer! There's no answer! I'm not implementing anything! But I think my classmates are having similair problems so it's mostly okay.

After that I have my Children's Lit Class and it is the BEST class ever!! My professor is the funniest guy ever and he thinks I'm a really good writer and he loves my analysis and all that stuff. The class goes on for forever and yet he manages to make things stay fresh and interesting. He's funny and he ends up pausing class to tell jokes or play games with us. Last class we read Where The Wild Things Are and he talked about how it might be the best childrens book ever written, which I'll buy. My older sister used to read it to me when we were young. This class honestly makes me feel very maternal (paternal? Naternal? I always forget which one is which), because half the class is just him talking about what kids want/need to hear and what stories appeal to them. It's sort of a child psychology class in that way. He said the Three Little Pigs is a story about how children will CONSTANTLY struggle and fail in life and only through never giving up can they get anywhere in life. He says Where The Wild Things Are is about man's struggle against his animalistic self, which he claims is a theme we see all the way back in the story of Gilgamesh and Enkidu.

I'm not feeling too prepared about my exam tommorow but I won't actually do anything about it. You just sorta have to hope when it comes to these kinds of things, ya know? I studied for the past two days, watch, ask me anything about anthropology. Genetic Drift is a random change in allele frequency that is best seen in smaller populations and Phenotype is observble traits that are influenced by genes and the environment. Plus, we're allowed one page of notes on the exam so I'll write all the things I don't remember like Autosomal Dominant and the Hardy Weinberg Equilibrium. Still, if I do fail this exam, it'll probably be pretty over for me, but that's no way to think.

I haven't made any friends in Anthropology. I'm actually kind of sort of offended! There was this girl who sat next to me on the first day and this guy who sat in front of me. I talked to both of them in the hopes of making friends! It was nice, casual, and quick stuff, "Hey what's your major," "How was your weekend," "Are you prepared for this class," like the most basic and inoffensive stuff in the world. Yet after a couple days of casually chatting with these two people, the girl started sitting on the OTHER SIDE OF THE ROOM ??? I didn't even do anything!! It was literally just like "Yo whats up" not like I was being super annoying or anything!! Everyone hates me and wants me to die. I have no friends in Children's Lit either but that's okay because my professor is my friend and I love him. Meanwhile in English, I'm friends with pretty much everyone who sits near me since it's a discussion class so we're always talking to each other. There's a boy who sits to the left of me, Luca, and he's always falling asleep in class but he's smart and a hard worker. There's a blonde girl who sits next to me, I think her name is Brook or something? She's nice but we've never really "talked" talked, just like the casual sort of "Hey, how's it going?" Then behind me sits Hazel, I love Hazel so so so so so much!! She threw up on my shows, tries to kiss everyone when she's drunk, and is very passionate about random things. She's an avid reader so I feel like I have to read more too to seem smart. I'm reading Dracula right now. Then further back is Cole, I do not like Cole, he seems like a very angry person. Next to Cole sits this girl who's name I don't know sorry, but she likes Invincible and Naruto so I think she's cool. In front of her is Holly and every time I hear her name I think of Hollie Sue from Yugioh. She is a nice person but just has the vibe of someone who wouldn't be my friend. Do you ever do that? I like to know my limits, certain people are just sort of out of reach. It's not as simple as "out of my league", that's more of a dating thing, but some people are just like... a little too human for me. In front of Hollie Sue sits Grace. I love love love Grace so much but I think she doesn't like me as much. We met at a party and I was very drunk so I don't remember all of it but I know she didn't really talk to me much after the party. The first two weeks of this class we didn't talk at all until I finally decided "Who cares? I can talk to whoever I want to talk to!" so I started talking to her. She's responsive and fun and nice and we joke and we laugh and she had some of the pie I made and said it was very good but I can't explain it, I can just feel like she doesn't like me. It's a little bit scary, like she's projecting an aura. Maybe she has a Stand Ability and I can't see it since I'm not a Stand User.

I don't have any friends in Chinese History class either, but once again it's the kind of class where the professor is my friend. He's a very sweet old man and he buys us snacks and drinks and he says we don't meet on Friday because "It's no good for people your age to be in a classroom all day, go out and flirt and drink and play sports!" Every project in that class is a group project so it's not like I don't know any people, we're just not friends, we're coworkers. Cow workers. Mooooooooooworkers.

I wanted to get like super deep and philosophical and talk about loneliness or something but I'm just not feeling it right now. Maybe it'll come to me later? Yeah I guess my first blog post shouldn't be too much of a bummer, so lets everyone positive thinking. That's all for now because I probably should go study. Though I feel like taking a nap instead, my head really hurts for some odd reason.
