A Nagging Sort of Feeling

4/24/26

Recently I sat and had breakfast with a girl, Hazel, from one of my classes. It was a chance encounter, I was walking around the dining hall disapointed that they weren't serving my usual breakfast (I hate change) and I saw her sitting there reading. Saturday. Almost exactly 7 days ago since then.

Something one must understand about me, my shyness and social ineptitude do NOT stem from a place of fearing cruelty from other people. I think highly of this girl, were I to bother or annoy her I believe that she is a good enough person that she wouldn't suddenly shout "Hey everyone! Come kill this person right here!" and tar and feather me while an angry crowd drags me away to the execution platform.

I do no tknow if I am unique in this thought process. I do not fear other people hurting me, other people hating me, or other people fearing me. I feel those would all be valid reactions and responses to my mere existence. So instead, my problem is I fear making people feel those things. Does that make sense? I'm not scared of someone being so scared or angry or uncomfortable that they decide to hate me forever, I'm scared of making someone feel scared or angry or uncomfortable. In my mind, that is the aura I project and as such I must hold myself back.

It's like I have a dark miasma that follows me around, like Judai from Yugioh GX and Yubel! He doesn't want to hurt people, but Yubel just doesn't understand his kindness and brings bad things upon everyone Judai interacts with! I have a dark spirit like that, obviously not literally, that'd be way to edgy, but metaphorically. There is some evil something, some cursed mark on my forehead or some toxic cloud or some evil spirit that evokes the worst in others. To keep them all safe, I keep myself at an arms distance. Gosh this is still sounding so edgy, I'M BEING METAPHORICAL I'M NOT SOME DUMB CHUUNIBYO WHO ACTUALLY THINKS THEY HAVE TO KEEP EVERYONE SAFE FROM THEIR EVIL WICKED OTHER HALF OR SOMETHING I SWEAR !!!

Essentially, what I'm getting at is this fear that there's something fundamentally wrong with me that other people dislike. Jeez. I probably should have just said that from the start instead of sounding like a geek. And so my fear isn't being disliked, it's making someone dislike me. There's a difference, I don't care too much about myself but I'm more worried about putting someone in an uncomfortable position of "Oh jeez I don't want to talk to this person at all but I don't want to be rude!" So it's like that basically. That's what has always kept me from trying to approach people, I'm scared of putting them on the spot and making them uncomfortable.

So at this point you're probably saying "But Lavenza, how did you end up eating breakfast with Hazel if this is the case?" And there is a simple explanation for it. When someone is desperate enough, they'll do things they normally wouldn't. It's been a couple weeks back from Spring Break and every time there's a break from school all I do is end up reflecting on how lonely I am. Everyone else is hanging out with friends, or going on dates, or seeing family, and I'm in my room playing Minecraft. It's just not fair but I fear it's my cosmic place of sorts. But then, our hero, Lavenza, has gone beyond their limits and reached a point of true desperation.

So I walked up to her and waved. She was reading and had headphones on, a clear and obvious "Don't talk to me aura" but I had absolutely reached a point of not caring. This is something that happens on occassion, a Zenkai Boost, I lose so badly and suffer so terribly and fall so deep that who cares what I do because things couldn't get any worse! And shockingly? She actually took off her headphones, put down the book, and smiled. We talked for quite a while, it was kind of frightening. Don't get me wrong, there was definetely awkward silence, but that's because this was like the first time we talked outside of class where she wasn't also drunk. It wasn't terrible. I look forward to talking her next Saturday too, my plan is to go to the gym at 9 am and then be in the dining hall by 11 which is when she's there too. I want to read a book too because seh reads and I want her to think I'm smart.

We sat across from each other at a small table in the dining hall, likely a bit too small. The food had cooled enough that eating was no longer a task but wasn't too enjoyable either. There was a droning, the constant buzz of a college dining hall. Music played too, but no songs I recognized. Sometimes they play Laufey or Beabadoobie and that always makes me happy.

She looked down at the table, glancing at her phone for a moment, before looking across the hall and out the window. Patio chairs waited next to a bubbling fountain. On occassions one could find ducks sitting by that fountain during the daytime, and at night raccoons can be found dipping their hands in the water.

Then, still without looking at me, she said my full name. (Obviously, for the sake of this blog, I'll be using my fake name.) "Lavenza Tea"

Not a question, she just sort of placed it out there.

"Yeah?" I looked up.

She wasn't snapped back to awarness, she continued to stare out the window for quite some time. Lingered off to the side, as if she were dreaming something no one else could see.

"I was just thinking," she answered sleepily, "I've been reading your name a lot recently, so it's kind of stuck in my head now." Like a song?

I nodded, unsure of exactly what one is supposed to say in a situation like this. "Yeah I... suppose that makes sense?"

She tilted her head, finally looking back to me, her a smile warming her face. "It feels like your name."

"I would hope so..."

"That's not what I mean," she shook her head softly, she seemed almost dissapointed. But honestly, I can't help but read into that a bit too much, like picking the wrong dialogue in a video game. A little 'X' popped up above her head, and misshapen lumpy music notes fall pathetically to the floor. "It's more like... I can't imagine you having a different name. Like you couldn't have a 'Lavenza' without a 'Tea'. And you couldn't have a 'Tea' without a 'Lavenza' either. It works both ways, and it only works because it's both."

It was completely lost on me. Was this just a roundabout way of saying "I like your name"? The more I tried to follow the line of thought, the more it slipped away from me. It left this nagging sort of feeling, a gnawing at the back of my mind. It didn't really even feel like a statement, more like, loose thoughts that trickled their way from her head. So I softly laughed, "Yeah, I guess it is my name?"

She looked at me, again with another dissapointed sort of look, almost a petulant kind of pout? More recently she pouted at me after I showed her a picture of a dead rat. I know that sounds bad but SHE ASKED ME TO and it wasn't a gorey photo, the little guy was just caught in a trap! Besides, it wasn't really an angry pout, it was a cute one and she told me to bake her another pie as an apology. Regardless (irregardless isn't grammatically correct, the "i" and the "less" are redundant. Regardless means "without regard", Iregard (if it were a word) would also mean "without regard", so irregardless means "without regard without". Just kidding, that's made up.) she looked at me like she was checking something, a list in her head maybe? Then she shook her head, "No. You don't get it."

I didn't. I searched for something to say back and just couldn't think of anything. Nothing fit, no joke, no rebuttal, no sarcastic remark. It felt like searching for an answer I didn't study for, my tongue seeking out words that maybe didn't exist yet.

The conversation picked back up, but that feeling never left my head. I don't think the interaction soured, we're still acquaintances, she waved at me just before I started writing this actually. But there's still some lingering, nagging feeling.
